Valle Nevado. Peak experience.

Valle Nevado, Chile’s largest ski resort, is 60 km from Santiago at 3,025 meters. It offers 40 km of slopes, modern lifts, and connects to La Parva and El Colorado. With 7 meters of snow per season, it’s a top spot for skiers and snowboarders. Known for heli-skiing, the resort provides access to untouched Andean terrain. Accommodation ranges from hotels to fine dining. Despite its size, it’s less crowded than resorts in North America and Europe, offering great skiing and stunning Andes views.

La Parva Challenging heights.

La Parva, 50 km from Santiago at 2,750 meters, is an exclusive ski resort favored by locals and elite skiers. It operates as a private village with mostly privately owned accommodations. Known for its steep, technical terrain and excellent off-piste access, it’s ideal for advanced skiers. The north-facing slopes offer longer daylight and views of Santiago. While there are fewer commercial facilities and nightlife, the resort’s quiet atmosphere, reliable snow, and fewer crowds make it perfect for a more relaxed, challenging ski experience.

El Colorado Snow playground.

El Colorado, located 36 km from Santiago at 3,333 meters, is the most accessible resort in Chile, ideal for families, beginners, and freestyle riders. It offers 112 runs across 1,100 hectares, with well-groomed slopes and a Snowpark for freestyle enthusiasts. Affordable accommodations make it a great option for budget-conscious visitors. Although it can get crowded on weekends, its variety of slopes and proximity to Santiago make it a popular choice for all skiers.

Nevados de Chillán Adventure awaits.

Nevados de Chillán, located in southern Chile, combines a top-class ski resort with natural thermal hot springs. With elevations up to 3,200 meters, it offers diverse terrain, deep powder, and excellent backcountry access. Known for its volcanic landscape and long ski season, it’s perfect for both beginners and expert skiers looking for a unique experience. The resort features luxury hotels and spa services, making it a great place to relax after skiing.

Portillo Legendary slopes.

Portillo, Chile’s most iconic ski resort, sits at 2,880 meters near the Argentina border. With a limited number of skiers allowed per day, it offers uncrowded runs and pristine conditions. Famous for its steep terrain and the stunning Laguna del Inca backdrop, Portillo is a favorite among professionals and ski teams worldwide. The all-inclusive hotel experience adds to its exclusivity, making it a must-visit destination.

Corralco Pure nature.

Corralco, located in the Malalcahuello National Reserve, offers a peaceful and scenic skiing experience surrounded by ancient Araucaria forests. With excellent snow quality and uncrowded slopes, it’s ideal for those seeking a quiet yet adventurous ski trip. The resort is well-known for backcountry skiing, long runs, and breathtaking views of Lonquimay Volcano.
